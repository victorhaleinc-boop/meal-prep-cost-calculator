What to include in a useful meal prep spreadsheet

Most meal prep spreadsheets fail because they track only recipes or only macros. A good template needs both nutrition and spend, otherwise you cannot tell whether a batch is actually worth repeating.

Column What to enter Why it matters
Meal Recipe or batch name Keeps repeat meals organized when you compare weeks.
Servings How many portions the batch makes Lets you turn batch totals into useful per-serving numbers.
Ingredient cost The dollar cost of what you actually used Shows the real batch cost, not just the grocery receipt total.
Calories and macros Total calories, protein, carbs, and fat Lets you compare budget meals against fitness targets.
Grocery notes Store, substitutions, or ingredients to restock Makes the next prep run faster and less wasteful.

How to use it

Simple weekly workflow

  1. List the meals you are likely to repeat this week.
  2. Enter the actual ingredient cost used for each batch, not the full container price unless you used the whole thing.
  3. Add total calories, protein, carbs, and fat for the full batch.
  4. Enter servings to see whether the meal still fits your budget and macro targets per portion.
  5. Keep a notes column for substitutions, cheaper protein swaps, or stores with better prices.

If you only need a one-time answer, the free calculator is faster. If you want a meal library, grocery budget view, and weekly planner in one file, use the workbook pack instead.
