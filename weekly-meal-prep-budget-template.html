Template columns

What to track in a weekly meal prep budget sheet

A useful weekly sheet needs to connect groceries, batches, and servings. If you only track total spend, you cannot tell which meal is helping or hurting the budget.

Column What to enter Why it matters
Week Date range or prep cycle name Keeps multiple budget runs organized when prices change.
Batch Main meal or snack you prepped Shows which recipes deserve a repeat.
Servings Usable portions from the batch Turns grocery spend into a practical cost-per-meal number.
Batch cost Total ingredient cost used for that batch Separates cheap staples from expensive proteins or add-ons.
Cost per serving Batch cost divided by servings Lets you compare meals on the same basis.
Protein per serving Protein grams in one portion Helps you judge whether the cheapest meal is still doing its job.
Restock notes Missing items, swaps, or store notes Makes the next prep cycle faster and less wasteful.

How to use it

Simple weekly workflow

  1. List every batch you expect to eat during the week.
  2. Enter the ingredient cost used for each batch, not the full package price unless you used it all.
  3. Fill in servings and protein per serving so you can compare value, not just raw spend.
  4. Flag the batches that missed budget or macro targets and replace them next week.
  5. Carry the winners forward into the paid workbook if you want a reusable meal library.

Use the free calculator when you need quick math for one recipe. Use this weekly sheet when you are planning a real grocery cycle and need to see the whole week in one place.
